Best first move

When in doubt, start with a Problem Snapshot.

The first job is not to sell the biggest package. The first job is to sort the mess, name the missing information, and tell you the smallest useful next step.

What do I send?

Send whatever you already have. Useful items include:

  • Photos or short videos
  • Measurements or rough dimensions
  • Screenshots, links, receipts, or product ideas
  • Text messages, quote examples, customer notes, or service lists
  • Shop/process notes, machine photos, part examples, cycle-time guesses, or workflow pain points

The first reply will ask only for what is missing.

Do I need perfect measurements?

No. Rough measurements are enough to start. If exact dimensions matter, the deliverable will clearly mark them as missing or assumed before you spend money, order parts, quote work, or build anything.

What if I do not know which package to choose?

Choose the closest option or start with a Problem Snapshot. The intake is built to route messy requests to the smallest useful package first.

How fast is turnaround?

Turnaround depends on the amount of input and how many assumptions need to be checked. Typical planning ranges are:

  • Snapshot: 1–3 business days after enough info is received
  • Project, cleanup, shop, or business packet: 3–7 business days
  • Shop / CNC planning, AI workflow, custom tools, or quote packets: quoted by scope

What happens after I submit?

Your request is sorted into the closest desk: Project, Shop, Business, Digital, Cleanup, Custom, Shop / CNC, or AI workflow. Then Highway 38 identifies the smallest useful package, asks for missing information if needed, and gives you the next step or quote path.

Do you do calls?

The normal workflow is written first: form, email, text-style notes, photos, screenshots, and files. Calls are not required for most starter packages. If a call would help, it can be treated as a scoped add-on instead of the default.

Can you work from photos?

Yes. Photos are often the best starting point. Marked-up photos, before/after notes, missing-measurement lists, zone plans, and first-action checklists are all built around photo-based input.

Can you help with CNC, robots, fixtures, or automation?

Yes, at the concept and planning level. Highway 38 can help organize the problem, map the process, prepare vendor questions, outline ROI assumptions, review fixture/inspection concepts, and build quote-ready packets.

Can you build the final automation system?

No final machine integration is promised from the website. Final robot programming, controls, guarding, electrical work, installation, and safety validation belong with qualified vendors and professionals.

Is this engineering, design, legal, tax, or permit advice?

No. Highway 38 provides practical planning, organization, workflow review, concept packets, trackers, checklists, and quote-prep support. It does not replace licensed engineering, architecture, legal, tax, permit, electrical, machine guarding, safety certification, or code-compliance signoff.
