Common questions

Clear expectations before work begins.

When does turnaround begin?

After required information is received, the scope and buying terms are confirmed, and the catalog’s payment or deposit requirement is satisfied.

What counts as a revision?

A revision adjusts the approved deliverable using the original goal and source information. A new output, space, machine, workflow, or materially changed requirement is a scope change.

What happens if the project changes?

The affected work pauses, the change is documented, and a revised scope or different approved product is prepared. Added work is never assumed automatically.

Are calls required?

No. The normal workflow uses the request guide, email, photos, screenshots, files, and structured questions. A call may be scoped only when it materially improves the result.

Can Highway 38 verify measurements?

No. Customer-supplied measurements are treated as supplied or assumed. Critical unknowns are marked before purchase, layout, or build decisions.

Do products include CAD files?

Only when a product explicitly lists that format. Basic layouts and concept reviews use annotated images or simple diagrams, not production CAD, sealed drawings, or permit-ready plans.

Can Highway 38 access my Google Drive or other tools?

Only after scope, permission, and access boundaries are approved. Original source material is preserved unless a specific approved action says otherwise.

Will files be deleted or moved?

Cleanup Rescue Plan does not move files. Cleanup Implementation follows an approved scope and preserves originals or an agreed working copy. Permanent deletion requires specific approval.

Does Highway 38 install automation equipment?

No. Manufacturing products provide planning, concepts, data, RFQ preparation, and decision support. Qualified providers own final engineering, controls, electrical, guarding, integration, installation, compliance, and safety validation.

Does Highway 38 select the final robot, camera, fixture, or controls?

No final selection authority is implied. The products define requirements, assumptions, questions, and acceptance criteria so qualified vendors can propose and validate the solution.

Is ROI guaranteed?

No. ROI products show assumptions, data quality, scenarios, and sensitivity. They do not guarantee savings, throughput, payback, uptime, or financial results.

How are customer files protected?

Only information needed for the agreed scope should be submitted. Credentials, tokens, private keys, payment information, and unnecessary sensitive records should never be entered into public forms.

What payment is required?

The exact payment rule shown in the approved product or bundle record controls. Planning products are generally prepaid; larger builds may use a deposit with the balance due before unrestricted final delivery.

What if information is incomplete?

Highway 38 asks only for missing information needed to continue. Critical assumptions and unresolved decisions remain visible.

What if the request is outside scope?

The request is declined, narrowed, or referred to an appropriate licensed or qualified professional. Highway 38 does not imply engineering, architecture, legal, tax, permit, electrical, guarding, safety, or code-compliance authority.

What happens after the first product?

You receive the finished result and a relevant upgrade only when deeper work would help. Buying the next product is optional.
