Common questions

Clear expectations before work begins.

When does turnaround begin?

After the required information and applicable payment or deposit are received and the scope is confirmed.

What counts as a revision?

A revision adjusts the approved deliverable using the original goal and source information. A new output, space, machine, workflow, or materially changed requirement is a scope change.

What happens if the project changes?

The affected work pauses, the change is documented, and a revised scope or different product is prepared. Added work is never assumed automatically.

Are calls required?

No. The normal workflow uses the request form, email, photos, screenshots, files, and structured questions. A call may be scoped when it materially improves the result.

Can Highway 38 verify measurements?

No. Customer-supplied measurements are treated as supplied or assumed. Critical unknowns are marked before purchase or build decisions.

Do products include CAD files?

Only when a product explicitly lists that format. Basic layouts and concept reviews use annotated images or simple diagrams, not production CAD or sealed drawings.

Can Highway 38 access my Google Drive?

Only after scope, permission, and access boundaries are approved. Original source material is preserved unless a specific approved action says otherwise.

Will files be deleted or moved?

Cleanup Rescue Plan does not move files. Cleanup Implementation uses an approved scope and preserves originals or an agreed working copy. Permanent deletion requires specific approval.

Does Highway 38 install automation equipment?

No. Manufacturing products provide planning, concepts, data, RFQ preparation, and decision support. Qualified vendors own final engineering, controls, electrical, guarding, integration, installation, and safety validation.

Does Highway 38 select the final robot, camera, fixture, or controls?

No final selection authority is implied. The products define requirements and questions so qualified vendors can propose and validate the solution.

Is ROI guaranteed?

No. ROI products show assumptions, data quality, scenarios, and sensitivity. They do not guarantee savings, throughput, payback, or financial results.

How are customer files protected?

Customer names, contact details, private links, credentials, tokens, internal message references, and private records remain outside public pages.

What payment is required?

Products below $500 generally require full payment before fulfillment. Larger products use the payment requirement shown in the controlled catalog. No payment request is sent without owner approval.

What if information is incomplete?

Highway 38 asks only for missing information needed to continue. Critical assumptions are not hidden.

What if the request is outside scope?

The request is declined or referred to an appropriate licensed or qualified professional. Highway 38 does not imply engineering, architecture, legal, tax, permit, electrical, guarding, safety, or code-compliance authority.

What happens after the first product?

The customer receives the finished result and a relevant upgrade only when the approved outcome shows that deeper work would help.
