FAQ

Clear expectations before work begins.

Answers for project planning, quoting, business systems, automation, measurements, revisions, payment, privacy, and professional boundaries.

What is the best way to start?

Use Start a Project and send what you already have: photos, notes, measurements, screenshots, files, constraints, and the result you want. Highway 38 identifies the smallest useful next step.

Does submitting a request create a charge?

No. Scope, price, timing, required information, boundaries, and payment terms are reviewed and approved before work begins.

Are calls required?

No. Most work can begin through photos, files, email, text, and structured questions. A conversation is used only when it materially improves the result.

Can Highway 38 verify measurements?

No. Customer measurements are treated as supplied or assumed. Critical dimensions and site conditions must be verified before purchasing, fabrication, permitting, or construction.

Are the examples customer projects?

Only items explicitly labeled verified proof should be treated as customer proof. Representative and hypothetical examples demonstrate workflow and deliverable style without claiming a real customer result.

What counts as a revision?

A revision adjusts the approved deliverable using the original goal and source information. A new space, output, machine, workflow, or materially changed requirement is a scope change.

Does Highway 38 provide permit, engineering, or sealed drawings?

No unless separately performed by an appropriately licensed professional. Highway 38 provides concepts, planning information, assumptions, questions, and professional-review triggers.

Does Highway 38 install automation equipment?

Not as part of planning work. Qualified providers own final engineering, controls, electrical, guarding, integration, installation, compliance, and safety validation.

Is ROI guaranteed?

No. ROI work documents assumptions, data quality, scenarios, and sensitivity. It does not guarantee savings, throughput, uptime, or payback.

How are files and private information handled?

Only submit information needed for the approved scope. Credentials, tokens, private keys, payment information, and unnecessary sensitive records should never be entered into public forms.

What happens if the project changes?

The affected work pauses, the change is documented, and a revised scope is prepared. Added work is never assumed automatically.

What if the request is outside Highway 38’s scope?

The request is narrowed, declined, or referred to a qualified professional. Highway 38 does not imply authority it does not have.
