Customer path

A clear six-step experience

1

Choose the desired outcome

Select what you want to have when the work is finished. No internal desk selection is required.

2

Send the rough information

Photos, screenshots, measurements, notes, files, links, process data, and known limits are enough to start.

3

Confirm product, scope, and price

Highway 38 identifies missing information and confirms the fixed product or bounded quote before work begins.

4

Build the deliverable

The approved build sheet controls inputs, assumptions, deliverables, exclusions, revision count, and proof requirements.

5

Review and QA

Goal, scope, privacy, links, assumptions, boundaries, and first action are checked before customer-facing delivery.

6

Receive and use the result

The customer receives the finished output, first action, open questions, and a relevant upgrade only when it helps.

Operating controls

Controls protect the customer and the owner.

Scope control

One product record defines required inputs, limits, deliverables, exclusions, turnaround, revisions, price, and completion criteria.

Owner approval

No unapproved customer email, quote send, payment request, final delivery, website deployment, or social publishing.

Proof and recovery

Important approved actions require proof records. Blocked, failed, unsafe, or uncertain actions require error records and review.

Formal approval statuses, selected-row execution, duplicate-send protection, Proof Log, and Error Log remain inside the operating system.
