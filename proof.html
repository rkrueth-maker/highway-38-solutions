Method and controls

Preservation controls ready. Available evidence indexed. Public summary compiled separately.

1. Preserve source when received

When the original archive is privately provided, it will be SHA-256 hashed, copied to a read-only working file, verified against the original, and extracted only from the working copy.

2. Index privately

Folders, dates, subject digests, sender-domain classes, attachment-name digests, extensions, and matched evidence groups are indexed. Raw message bodies and attachment contents are not written into the public index.

3. Review proof

Each candidate receives a classification, corroboration status, privacy status, source count, public wording, boundary, and unresolved-question list.

4. Review photos

Photos are classified as before, during, after, reference, or unknown. People, addresses, family, customers, vendors, employees, drawings, prices, plates, screens, mail, and metadata cause redaction or hold.

5. Compile public-safe only

The public registry accepts only explicit public-safe summaries. Reconstructions are never presented as verified customer or employer case studies.

6. Keep questions visible

Missing source files, uncertain dates, unverified quantities, permissions, and provider limitations remain recorded instead of being silently waived.
